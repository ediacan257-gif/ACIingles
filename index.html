Unit 5: The Evolution of Play & Perception

Beyond the Screen: The Science of Pro-Gaming

In the past, people couldn’t imagine that playing video games would become a professional career. However, the human brain is more adaptable than we thought. Pro-gamers today don’t just play; they train their senses to a level that normal people can't reach without practice.

When a professional player is in a competition, they must react more instantly than a casual player. Their eyes move more rapidly across the screen, identifying threats through peripheral vision. This is related to our sense of sight, but also to "proprioception" (the sense of self-movement and body position).

In terms of cognitive ability, a pro-gamer can process information more efficiently because their brain creates shortcuts. While a beginner might think "I need to press the X button now", a pro does it automatically. Interestingly, research shows that playing strategic video games can help people perform better in real-life tasks, such as driving or performing surgery, because they can handle stress more calmly than others.

As games evolve into Virtual Reality (VR), our senses of balance and touch are becoming more important. In the future, we will be able to feel the texture of objects in a digital world. The question is: if our senses are perfectly fooled, can we still distinguish between what is real and what is "just a game"?
